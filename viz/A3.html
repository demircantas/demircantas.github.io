Visualization Sketches

We split the spiral into two separate years and flatten it into a horizontal chart for easier reading of magnitude.

  • We change the range of the plot so that it begins on July and ends on June of the following year. This creates a better frame for the dramatic increase of cases in January 2022.
  • We add dashed / ghosted lines to the beginning and the end of plots to emphasize the continuity.
  • We add annotations marking important events during the pandemic.

The spiral concept communicates how the pandemic has the potential to spiral out of control due to the Omicron variant.

  • We implement a fixed radius spiral in a 3 dimensional space to keep magnitude consistent among years.
  • We create a highlight structure to zoom into significant events in the chart.
  • This approach does not reach its full potential in a 2 dimensional medium. However, In a VR application, it would equip the user with the ability to assume different viewpoints that make comparisons easier. We would implement preset perspectives to emphasize significant events in comparison.

We implement a circular representation for each year instead of a continous spiral.

  • Separating the years fixes the scale problem with the spiral design.
  • We solve continuity via the use of extended dashed / ghosted lines at the beginning and end of each year, connected via highlighted arrows.
  • We create combs under the lines (see orange lines in the figure). The combs combine the line plot with bar chart elements. Furthermore, we can color the combs with additional data like the severity of the cases, or the rate of increase in case reports.
